Frequently Asked Questions

Is investing safe for teenagers?

Investing carries risk. Teenagers can start learning and use simulations or custodial accounts guided by parents. Focus on education and low-cost diversified funds for real investing.

How much should I start with?

Even small amounts help you learn. Aim to save regularly. The exact amount depends on your goals.

What's the difference between saving and investing?

Saving is setting money aside (often low risk, easy access). Investing aims for growth but can fluctuate in value.

Where can I practice without risk?

Use paper portfolios, stock market simulators, or demo accounts that let you practice with virtual money.

Who should I ask for help?

Talk to parents, teachers, or a qualified financial advisor for big decisions. For learning, use trusted educational resources.
