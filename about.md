## Our Mission

InvestEd was created with a simple belief: every young person deserves access to quality financial education. We make investment knowledge accessible to teenagers and young adults through the Cambridge ACE and A-Level curriculum, turning what can feel like an intimidating world into something approachable, practical, and even exciting. Financial literacy is not just an academic subject; it is a life skill that empowers you to take control of your future, make informed decisions, and build lasting security.

## Who This Is For

- **Cambridge ACE (A/Advanced Equivalent) students** aged 14 to 21 who are exploring personal finance for the first time
- **College and university students** looking to strengthen their understanding of investing and wealth-building
- **Teachers and parents** who want to guide young learners through foundational financial concepts
- **Anyone curious** about how investing works, regardless of background or experience

## How to Use This Site

Follow this learning journey at your own pace:

1. **Start with Learning** — Read our structured lessons that take you from the very basics to more advanced investment concepts, all aligned with ACE/A-Level standards.
2. **Take the Quiz** — Test your knowledge across three difficulty levels and see how much you have learned.
3. **Explore Investment Plans** — Find a personalized roadmap that matches your age, budget, and goals.
4. **Discover Ideas** — Get actionable investment ideas for every budget, from zero-ksh start-up funds to larger portfolios.
5. **Browse the FAQ** — Quick answers to common questions about investing, safety, tools, and more.

## The Curriculum Connection

This content is designed to complement your ACE/A-Level studies across multiple subjects:

- **Mathematics** — Real-world applications of compound interest, percentage calculations, data analysis, and probability.
- **Business Studies** — Understanding how markets work, how companies are valued, and the principles of economic growth.
- **Economics** — Exploring supply and demand, inflation, monetary policy impacts, and the role of financial institutions.
- **Critical Thinking** — Evaluating investment risks and opportunities, comparing strategies, and making evidence-based decisions.

## Acknowledgements

InvestEd was created as a free educational resource for the Cambridge ACE student community. We are grateful to educators, students, and mentors who contributed ideas and feedback during development. This project aims to fill a gap in accessible financial education for young learners across Kenya and beyond.

**Disclaimer:** The content on this website is provided for educational purposes only. It is not financial advice. Always do your own research or consult a qualified financial advisor before making investment decisions.
